Leaving Hanoi

My Bags are Packed
Nov 10, 2023 4 min read
1300

I went to the gym for an hour this morning. I missed yesterday's workout so I did two workout days in one. I did most of what I'd do in two days. Then, I went back to the Hive Homestay and repacked. I managed to get rid of a few more items and now I've got everything packed comfortable into 2 bags. I'm totally hunter gatherer. All my life possessions reduced to a medium sized suitcase, a carry-on size and a backpack. Here we go again...

I was going through a stack of important documents; not much more than I'd like to take. I found papers back from 2005 - almost 20 years ago. I thought they were important but a couple Nakhorn read they're going to hire me at Benjama. I had this rubbish with me for this long and thought they were official because of the red stamps. I guess I didn't bother reading them up until this morning.

I felt like an idiot. I thought about the work I'd done and the jobs I got. The way I dressed and the way I behaved to peers and students. Who did I think I was? Vietnam has made me see that I'm nothing more than a laborer, basically a slave, and I'm walking around telling them how they can't treat me.

God, Please direct me into right thinking.

1500

I was also reading work contracts, thinking I didn't care much about getting paid but was grateful for being given a job where I can work on my professional skills. They should have been paying me. This is how Musicians feel when years later they realize how their manager had swindled them. I heard Steven Tyler talking about this on the Rogan show. It was only after the band got sober that they regretted signing papers while their manager was getting them high.

Tom Evans
